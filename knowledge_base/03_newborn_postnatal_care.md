# Newborn & Postnatal Care — Field Reference

Source basis: synthesized from standard national postnatal/newborn care guideline structure, for demo/eval purposes only.

## Essential Newborn Care (First Hour)

1. Immediate drying and skin-to-skin contact
2. Delayed cord clamping (1–3 minutes after birth, unless resuscitation needed)
3. Initiation of breastfeeding within first hour
4. Vitamin K injection
5. Weighing and basic examination
6. Eye care (antimicrobial ointment per protocol)

## Postnatal Visit Schedule (Mother & Baby)

| Visit | Timing |
|---|---|
| PNC 1 | Within 24 hours of delivery (or immediately if home birth) |
| PNC 2 | Day 3 |
| PNC 3 | Day 7 |
| PNC 4 | Day 42 (6 weeks) |

## Newborn Danger Signs (Immediate Referral)

- Not feeding / refusing to feed
- Convulsions
- Fast breathing (>60 breaths/min) or grunting
- Severe chest indrawing
- Temperature <35.5°C or >37.5°C
- Lethargy / reduced movement / floppy baby
- Yellowing of skin on palms/soles (severe jaundice)
- Umbilical redness/pus/foul smell
- Multiple skin pustules

## Maternal Postpartum Counseling Points

- Exclusive breastfeeding for first 6 months
- Family planning counseling can begin at first PNC visit
- Perineal/wound hygiene if applicable
- Watch for signs of postpartum depression: persistent sadness, inability to bond, extreme anxiety beyond 2 weeks — refer for mental health support
- Nutrition: continue IFA and calcium supplementation through 6 months postpartum

## Kangaroo Mother Care (for low birth weight babies <2000g)

- Continuous or near-continuous skin-to-skin contact
- Exclusive breastfeeding
- Can be initiated once baby is stable, even before target weight reached
- Should be continued at home with family support and follow-up visits
