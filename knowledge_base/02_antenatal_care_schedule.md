# Antenatal Care (ANC) Schedule — Field Reference

Source basis: synthesized from standard national ANC guideline structure, for demo/eval purposes only.

## Recommended Minimum ANC Visits

| Visit | Timing | Key Checks |
|---|---|---|
| ANC 1 | As soon as pregnancy confirmed, ideally within first trimester | Registration, blood pressure, weight, hemoglobin, blood group, urine test, HIV/syphilis screening, LMP & EDD calculation |
| ANC 2 | 14–26 weeks | BP, weight, fundal height, fetal heart sound (after ~20 weeks), abdominal exam, IFA supply |
| ANC 3 | 28–34 weeks | BP, weight, fundal height, fetal position check, danger sign counseling, TT/Td vaccination |
| ANC 4 | 36 weeks to delivery | BP, weight, fetal presentation, birth-preparedness counseling, referral plan confirmation |

Additional visits are recommended for high-risk pregnancies (twins, prior C-section, gestational diabetes, hypertension, anemia, age <18 or >35, short birth spacing).

## Birth Preparedness Checklist (to counsel families on)

- Identified facility for delivery
- Transport plan arranged in advance
- Blood donor identified if anemia present
- Emergency fund set aside
- Postnatal care and newborn care plan discussed

## Nutrition Counseling Points

- Increase caloric intake by ~300 kcal/day in 2nd and 3rd trimester
- Iron-folic acid: 1 tablet/day for minimum 180 days during pregnancy
- Calcium: 1 tablet/day (500mg) from 14 weeks, for 180 days
- Avoid raw/undercooked food, unpasteurized dairy
- Adequate hydration and rest

## Tetanus/Td Vaccination

- Td1: as early as possible in pregnancy
- Td2: at least 4 weeks after Td1
- If previously vaccinated within last 3 years, only a booster dose is needed

## When ANC Visits Should Be More Frequent (High-Risk Criteria)

- Age below 18 or above 35 years
- Height below 145 cm
- History of stillbirth, miscarriage, or C-section
- Multiple pregnancy (twins/triplets)
- Pre-existing diabetes, hypertension, heart disease, or thyroid disorder
- Severe anemia (Hb < 7 g/dL)
- Rh-negative blood group
