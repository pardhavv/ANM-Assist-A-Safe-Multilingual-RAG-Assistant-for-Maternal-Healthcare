# Danger Signs During Pregnancy — Field Reference

Source basis: synthesized from standard maternal-health protocol categories (ICMR/WHO-aligned) for demo/eval purposes. NOT for real clinical use.

## High-Risk Danger Signs (Immediate Referral Required)

1. **Severe vaginal bleeding** at any stage of pregnancy — soaking more than one pad per hour, or bleeding with clots.
2. **Severe headache with blurred vision** — especially after 20 weeks, may indicate pre-eclampsia.
3. **High blood pressure** — systolic ≥ 140 mmHg or diastolic ≥ 90 mmHg on two readings, especially combined with swelling of face/hands.
4. **Convulsions or fits** — possible eclampsia. This is a medical emergency requiring immediate transport to a facility with obstetric care.
5. **High fever (>38°C) with chills** — possible infection or sepsis.
6. **Severe abdominal pain** — not relieved by rest, may indicate placental abruption or ectopic pregnancy (early stages).
7. **Reduced or absent fetal movement** — after 28 weeks, if the mother reports fewer than 10 movements in 2 hours.
8. **Draining of watery fluid from vagina** before 37 weeks — possible premature rupture of membranes.
9. **Severe vomiting with inability to keep fluids down** for more than 24 hours — risk of dehydration.
10. **Difficulty breathing or chest pain.**

## Moderate-Risk Signs (Monitor Closely, Refer if Persistent)

- Mild swelling of feet/ankles (common; concerning if it spreads to face/hands)
- Mild-to-moderate back pain
- Occasional Braxton-Hicks contractions before term
- Mild nausea in first trimester
- Urinary frequency without pain or burning

## Escalation Protocol for Frontline Workers

- Any **High-Risk** sign → escalate immediately to the referral facility / supervising doctor; do not attempt home management.
- **Moderate-Risk** signs → advise rest, hydration, and a follow-up visit within 48–72 hours; escalate if symptoms worsen or persist beyond 3 days.
- When in doubt, always escalate. False positives (unnecessary referrals) are preferable to missed emergencies.

## Anemia Screening

- Hemoglobin < 11 g/dL is classified as anemia in pregnancy.
- < 7 g/dL is severe anemia and requires immediate referral for possible transfusion evaluation.
- Iron-folic acid (IFA) supplementation is standard: 1 tablet daily from confirmation of pregnancy through 6 months postpartum, per national programs.

## Postpartum Danger Signs (First 48 Hours)

1. Heavy bleeding (soaking a pad in <30 minutes) — possible postpartum hemorrhage, life-threatening.
2. Fever with foul-smelling vaginal discharge — possible puerperal sepsis.
3. Severe headache or visual disturbance — possible late-onset eclampsia.
4. Baby not breastfeeding / very lethargic / poor cry — refer newborn urgently.
5. Baby with yellowing of skin (jaundice) spreading to limbs — refer for bilirubin check.
